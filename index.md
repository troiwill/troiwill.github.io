I am currently a Computer Science Ph.D. candidate in the [Robot Perception and Action Lab](https://rpal.cse.usf.edu) at The University of South Florida (Tampa, FL USA). I work under the advisement of Dr. Yu Sun, Professor of Computer Science and Engineering. Since I began my Ph.D. program, I have been awarded grants, scholarships, and fellowships from Microsoft Research, Tapia, NSF Bridge to Doctorate, McKnight, GEM Consortium, and Sloan.

My research develops methods for learning sensor measurement models that predict the measurement likelihood for a sensor. My research helps a robot dynamically predict the expected bias and uncertainty of its sensors, which broadly affects autonomous robotics research (including cars and aerial vehicles).
